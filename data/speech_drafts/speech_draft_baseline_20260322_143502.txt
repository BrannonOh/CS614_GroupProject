Have you ever wondered how Singapore keeps its lights on despite global conflicts happening thousands of miles away? It’s a question worth asking, especially when you consider just how much we rely on energy coming from beyond our shores. Singapore imports over 95% of its electricity needs through natural gas, making us heavily dependent on external sources. This reliance means that disruptions—like conflicts in the Middle East—can ripple through global markets and affect both the supply and the price of energy here at home. Understanding these risks is crucial. Having seen the risks, let's explore how Singapore remains resilient despite these challenges.

To safeguard our energy supply, Singapore has adopted a multi-pronged strategy. We’ve diversified both our energy sources and our suppliers to avoid over-dependence on any single region. For instance, natural gas is imported from multiple countries, including Indonesia and Malaysia, through pipelines and liquefied natural gas (LNG) shipments. This diversification helps us spread risk and maintain steady supplies even if one source faces disruption. Beyond just supply routes, Singapore has invested in strong infrastructure and maintains strategic reserves that act as buffers against short-term shocks in the market or supply chain. These measures collectively strengthen our resilience. Beyond current resilience, Singapore is actively shaping a more secure energy future.

Looking ahead, Singapore is also investing heavily in sustainable and low-carbon energy solutions to enhance our security further. One key initiative is importing renewable energy such as solar power from regional grids, which reduces reliance on fossil fuels and diversifies our energy mix even more. Additionally, efforts are underway to develop low-carbon alternatives like hydrogen and to improve overall energy efficiency across sectors. These innovations are critical as we aim to meet future demand while reducing environmental impact. In fact, Singapore plans to import up to 4 gigawatts of low-carbon electricity by 2035—this would cover about 30% of our projected electricity demand. Such ambitious goals underscore our commitment to a secure and sustainable energy future. But energy security is not just about infrastructure and technology; it involves everyone.

Energy security isn’t solely the responsibility of the government or industry; households and businesses have a vital role too. Since households account for a significant share of electricity consumption, conservation efforts at home can make a real difference when scaled across the nation. Building awareness about responsible energy use encourages everyone to contribute toward strengthening national resilience. Simple actions like turning off unused appliances or choosing energy-efficient devices collectively help reduce demand pressures and enhance overall security for all of us. Finally, let's consider how Singapore balances security, sustainability, and cost in its energy policies.

Navigating the future requires a careful balancing act—ensuring that our energy supply is reliable while also meeting environmental goals and keeping costs affordable for everyone. This challenge is often called the “energy trilemma,” where reliability, cost, and sustainability must all be managed simultaneously without compromising one for another. Long-term planning is essential so that Singapore remains secure even amid ongoing global uncertainties—from geopolitical tensions to climate change impacts. By thoughtfully balancing these priorities today, we lay the groundwork for a stable energy future tomorrow. In conclusion, Singapore’s comprehensive approach secures our energy future for all.

To sum up, Singapore’s energy security depends on three key pillars: diversification of sources, continuous innovation in sustainable technologies, and active participation by all members of society in responsible consumption. Together—as individuals, communities, businesses, and government—we can ensure that our nation enjoys a resilient, sustainable, and affordable energy future for generations to come. Thank you.
