Good evening, everyone.

Let’s start with something striking. Just five years ago, in 2020, artificial intelligence struggled to solve basic math problems reliably. Fast forward to 2025, and AI can compete in the International Math Olympiad — even performing at a gold medal level. That’s a leap few would have predicted so quickly. Here in Singapore, AI is no longer some distant technology; it’s part of our daily lives. Our civil service uses AI in call centres to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and generate summaries, freeing officers to better serve you. And when Singaporeans plan their travels, generative AI usage has nearly doubled—from 11% to 20% in just one year. AI has transformed dramatically in a few years, becoming a powerful tool integrated into everyday activities. This rapid progress opens new opportunities for Singapore’s economy and workforce, which we will explore next.

Across our island, AI is boosting productivity and creating real value for businesses of all sizes. Take Tuas Port: it combines AI with robotics and autonomous machines, including a “Next Generation Vessel Traffic Management System” that optimizes ship movements and berth allocation efficiently. At Changi Airport, AI-powered autonomous tractors transport baggage, lifting manpower productivity on the airside. Even small and medium enterprises are benefiting—Q&M Dental uses AI to analyze dental X-rays more accurately, spotting fillings and tooth decay with greater precision. Big players are investing too: Amazon Web Services plans to pump an extra $9 billion into Singapore’s AI ecosystem between 2024 and 2029. This blend of technology and human expertise attracts investments and creates quality jobs here at home. And importantly, these benefits aren’t limited to large corporations; SMEs are right there alongside them. While AI brings growth, it also raises concerns about jobs, which we will address next.

It’s natural to worry about what new technology means for work—history shows us why. Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with computer skills; this transformed our economy. Then in the 1990s came Singapore ONE—one of the world’s first nationwide broadband networks—which propelled us into the digital age ahead of many others. Technological change has always caused disruption but ultimately enabled workers to move into higher-skilled, better-paying roles. Building on this history, the government is committed to supporting Singaporeans through skills development and job access.

That commitment is clear in action today. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above $4,000 in SkillsFuture credits plus training allowances of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to two years. Vinoth Nanda Kumaran is a great example: at 41 in 2025, he shifted from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits for a diploma in data engineering—and he’s continuing his studies in Business Analytics at SUSS. The government also works closely with unions through NTUC’s Company Training Committees to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills. Efforts include expanded job matching services, career fairs, and initiatives right down to town level. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme is expanding too—the Mid-Career Training Allowance now supports part-time learners for the first time as well. With these supports in place, Singapore is ready to embrace AI for a dynamic and competitive future.

Looking ahead, the numbers speak volumes: AI adoption alone is projected to add up to S$198.3 billion to Singapore’s economy by 2030. A dynamic economy like this creates good jobs and better lives for all Singaporeans—not just some of us but everyone who calls this country home. Embracing AI isn’t optional if we want to stay globally competitive; it’s essential. Our goal is clear: Singapore will lead through ideas, innovation, and unity—standing together as we shape a future that works for all.

Thank you.
