Good morning. Let’s start with something striking. Just a few years ago, the idea of artificial intelligence solving the toughest math problems at the International Mathematical Olympiad was science fiction. Yet in 2024, Google DeepMind’s AI models hit gold-medal standards there—something we couldn’t have imagined before. And it’s not just about complex math. More than half of Singaporean travellers—55% to be exact—now rely on AI tools for planning trips, getting personalized recommendations, and crafting itineraries. Even our civil service has jumped on board, using AI in call centres to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and generate summaries. This frees up officers to focus on what really matters: serving the public.

AI has transformed dramatically in just a few years, moving from a distant concept to a practical tool woven into daily life here in Singapore. This rapid evolution opens new opportunities for our economy and workforce.

Now, look at how AI is shaking things up across industries. At Tuas Port, AI teams up with robotics and autonomous machines to boost efficiency. Over at Changi Airport, they’re studying automation possibilities that could change how operations run. GE Vernova uses AI right here in Singapore to detect turbine anomalies and help human operators fix problems faster. And it’s not just the big players—SMEs like Q&M Dental are leveraging AI to analyze dental X-rays with greater accuracy.

The numbers tell the story: AI adoption among SMEs more than tripled from 4.2% in 2023 to 14.5% this year. Large companies aren’t standing still either—adoption jumped from 44% to 62.5%. Singapore’s blend of technology and human expertise is attracting quality investments and creating good jobs across the board—not just for big corporations but for smaller businesses too.

Understanding these real-world applications helps us see how AI can reshape jobs and industries right here at home.

But I know what some of you are thinking: What about job losses? History shows us that technological change shakes things up—but it doesn’t leave workers behind.

Take the 1980s national computerisation drive led by the National Computer Board. It was a deliberate move to boost Singapore’s economic competitiveness through technology. Then came public internet access in 1994, sparking rapid adoption—household computer ownership jumped from 36% in 1996 to 74% by 2005.

At PSA, crane operators didn’t lose their jobs; they were retrained to remotely manage multiple cranes simultaneously, improving productivity while upgrading job quality.

Yes, technology disrupts jobs—but it also creates better ones, with higher skills and pay.

This history of adaptation is our guide as we face AI-driven change today.

And we’re not leaving anyone behind this time either. The government works closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills for an AI future.

Consider the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme—it gives Singaporeans aged 40 and above a $4,000 credit top-up plus a monthly training allowance of up to $3,000 for full-time study. Vinoth Nanda Kumaran is one example: he moved from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer using SkillsFuture credits and further studies at SUSS.

NTUC’s Company Training Committee Grant has supported over 70 AI-focused projects—including one with SBS Transit using AI for predictive bus maintenance that upskills technicians.

Job matching services have expanded too, with career fairs helping Singaporeans tap into new opportunities.

These efforts make sure Singaporeans are prepared—and supported—to thrive alongside AI.

Looking ahead, Singapore ranks second globally in AI readiness and is investing over S$1 billion into AI research under our National AI Strategy 2.0.

A dynamic economy creates good jobs and better lives for all Singaporeans.

Embracing AI isn’t optional—it’s key if we want to stay competitive on the global stage.

Singapore aims not just to follow but to lead—with ideas, innovation, and unity driving us forward together.

Thank you.
