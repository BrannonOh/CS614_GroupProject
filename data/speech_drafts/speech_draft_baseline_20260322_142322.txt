Have you ever wondered how secure Singapore’s energy supply really is amid global conflicts? It’s a question worth asking, especially when you consider just how dependent we are on imported energy. Singapore imports over 95% of its electricity needs through natural gas, making us heavily reliant on external sources. But with ongoing global conflicts, such as those in the Middle East, the stability of supply and prices can be seriously threatened. These disruptions pose real risks to our energy security and, by extension, our daily lives and economy. Having established the risks, let's explore how Singapore remains resilient despite these challenges.

Singapore has taken deliberate steps to reduce these risks by diversifying both our energy sources and suppliers. We don’t rely on any single region for our natural gas; instead, we import from multiple countries including Indonesia and Malaysia. These imports come through a combination of pipelines and liquefied natural gas shipments, creating a more flexible and secure supply chain. Beyond diversification, Singapore has also invested in strong infrastructure and maintains strategic reserves to buffer against short-term shocks in supply or price fluctuations. This layered approach helps ensure that even when global events disrupt markets, our energy system remains stable. Building on this resilience, Singapore is also investing in a more secure energy future.

Looking ahead, Singapore is actively pursuing renewable and low-carbon energy solutions to strengthen our energy security further. One key strategy is importing renewable energy—such as solar power—from regional grids. This approach not only diversifies our energy mix but also supports sustainability goals. Additionally, there are ongoing efforts to develop low-carbon alternatives like hydrogen and to improve overall energy efficiency across sectors. By 2035, Singapore aims to import up to 4 gigawatts of low-carbon electricity, which would account for about 30% of our projected demand. These investments are critical as they reduce reliance on fossil fuels and make our energy system more resilient to future uncertainties. While technology and infrastructure are vital, the role of individuals and society is equally important.

Energy security isn’t just a government issue; it’s something every household and business contributes to through conservation efforts. Households alone account for a significant share of electricity consumption in Singapore, meaning that responsible usage can have a powerful impact at scale. By building awareness around energy conservation—turning off unused appliances, using energy-efficient devices, or simply being mindful of consumption—we collectively strengthen national resilience against supply disruptions or price spikes. This shared responsibility complements government initiatives perfectly and makes our entire system more robust. Finally, let's consider how Singapore balances security, sustainability, and cost in its energy policies.

Singapore faces the complex challenge of balancing reliable energy supply with environmental goals and affordability—a dynamic often called the “energy trilemma.” Our policies must ensure that electricity remains dependable so homes and businesses can function smoothly while also meeting ambitious climate targets and keeping costs manageable for consumers. Long-term planning is essential here; it allows us to anticipate global uncertainties and adapt accordingly without compromising any one priority. By carefully navigating these trade-offs today, Singapore ensures it stays secure well into the future despite an unpredictable world. In conclusion, Singapore’s multifaceted approach ensures energy security despite global challenges.

To sum up, Singapore’s energy security rests on strategic diversification, innovation in low-carbon technologies, strong infrastructure, and most importantly—collective responsibility from both government and citizens alike. Together, through thoughtful planning and shared commitment to conservation and sustainability, we can build a resilient energy future that withstands global disruptions while supporting economic growth and environmental stewardship. Let us all play our part in securing Singapore’s energy tomorrow—because it truly takes all of us working together to keep the lights on today and beyond. Thank you.
