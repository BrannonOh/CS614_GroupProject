It’s truly remarkable to witness how far artificial intelligence has come in such a short time. Not long ago, AI struggled with the simplest of tasks. Today, it solves problems that were once thought to be at the pinnacle of human intellect. This leap in capability has been exponential. Right here in Singapore, AI is no longer a distant or abstract concept — it is very much a part of our daily lives and public services. For instance, an AI model developed locally recently achieved gold medal-level performance at the International Mathematical Olympiad, a challenge once considered nearly impossible for machines. Meanwhile, in our public sector, AI powers call centres that understand and transcribe conversations in Singlish and all four official languages, providing real-time summaries to help officers serve citizens better. This rapid progress opens new opportunities for Singapore to harness AI across industries to boost productivity and create value.

By integrating AI with human expertise, Singapore is creating a powerful synergy that enhances productivity, attracts global investment, and generates high-value jobs. The AI revolution isn’t reserved just for large corporations; it’s empowering Small and Medium Enterprises to innovate and compete on a much larger scale. Take Tuas Port — one of the busiest ports in the world — where AI coordinates fleets of autonomous vehicles and robotic cranes, dramatically improving efficiency and safety. At GE Vernova’s aircraft engine repair facility, AI-powered systems scan turbine blades for microscopic defects invisible to the naked eye, enabling technicians to carry out more precise repairs critical for flight safety. Even local SMEs like Q&M Dental use AI tools that analyse X-rays to detect cavities earlier and more accurately, improving patient care outcomes. While AI brings exciting growth prospects, it also raises concerns about job displacement that we must address thoughtfully.

We understand these concerns deeply because history shows us that technological revolutions often cause short-term disruption but ultimately create new, higher-value roles for those who adapt and upskill. Singapore has a proven track record of navigating such transitions successfully. During the 1980s computerisation drive and the 1990s internet boom, we invested heavily in training our workforce — shifting from manual labour towards knowledge-based roles. At our port, for example, crane operators who once worked high above ground were retrained as remote supervisors managing multiple automated cranes from comfortable control centres — leading not only to better working conditions but also higher pay. Building on this experience, Singapore is prioritising support for workers so they can thrive in the AI era through targeted initiatives.

One key approach is our tripartite partnership with unions and employers to proactively redesign jobs that incorporate AI tools while ensuring workers’ skills remain relevant. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme exemplifies this commitment by providing mid-career Singaporeans with both financial support and training opportunities that enable meaningful career transformations. This programme offers individuals aged 40 and above $4,000 in SkillsFuture Credit plus a monthly training allowance for selected full-time courses. Consider Vinoth Nanda Kumaran’s inspiring journey: he used his SkillsFuture credits to transition from being a private hire driver into a Business Intelligence Developer — a high-demand role created by today’s data economy. To date, over 170,000 training places in AI-related courses have been facilitated by the government as part of preparing our workforce for the future. These efforts ensure Singaporeans are ready not just to adapt but to lead in an AI-driven world.

Our vision is clear: we aim not only for economic growth but inclusive prosperity where AI-driven innovation creates good jobs and improves quality of life for every Singaporean. In a world reshaped by artificial intelligence, our choice is not whether we adopt it but how we lead with it — maintaining our global competitiveness and relevance on the world stage. Through courage, creativity, and an unwavering commitment to leaving no one behind, we will harness AI’s potential to build a smarter, stronger, and more united Singapore. Our National AI Strategy 2.0 sets ambitious goals — including tripling our pool of AI practitioners to 15,000 over the next five years — ensuring we cultivate a critical mass of local talent ready to drive this transformation forward.

Together, let us embrace this future with confidence and purpose.

Thank you.
