Good evening, fellow Singaporeans.

Today, I will talk about artificial intelligence, or AI, and how Singapore can stay competitive. AI is something that many countries are discussing, and it is becoming more important over time. Singapore, as a small country, must pay attention to such developments if we want to continue to succeed.

Artificial intelligence is not something entirely new. Over the years, we have seen different kinds of technology emerge. For example, computers, the internet, and mobile phones have all changed how people live and work. AI is simply another step in this process. It allows machines to process data and make decisions in ways that are similar to humans.

In recent years, AI has improved a lot. In 2020, AI systems had difficulty solving basic math problems. But by 2025, AI was able to perform at a very high level, even reaching gold medal-level performance in the International Math Olympiad. This shows that AI is improving very quickly, and we must take this seriously.

In Singapore, AI is already being used in many areas. For example, people use AI for travel planning, where it can suggest destinations and generate itineraries. This makes it more convenient for users. In the civil service, AI is also used in call centres to transcribe conversations and generate summaries. This allows officers to spend less time on administrative work and more time serving the public.

These examples show that AI is useful, and it can improve efficiency. Therefore, Singapore must adopt AI if we want to remain competitive. If we do not do so, other countries may move ahead of us.

At the same time, AI is also being used in industries. For example, at Tuas Port, AI is combined with robotics and autonomous machines. At Changi Airport, AI is being studied for automation. These developments can improve productivity and efficiency. In addition, companies like GE Vernova use AI to detect anomalies in turbine components. This helps operators identify problems more easily.

Small and medium enterprises can also benefit from AI. For example, Q&M Dental uses AI to analyse dental X-rays. This helps detect issues such as tooth decay and impacted teeth more accurately. This shows that AI is not only for large companies but can also be used by smaller businesses.

All these examples show that AI can improve productivity. However, there are also concerns about jobs. Some people worry that AI will replace workers and lead to unemployment. These concerns are understandable because technology has changed jobs in the past.

However, history shows that technological change does not always lead to job losses. In the 1980s, Singapore introduced computerisation, which helped workers learn new skills. In the 1990s, the internet was introduced, and Singapore adopted it successfully. These changes helped the economy grow.

Similarly, AI may change jobs, but it can also create new opportunities. For example, at PSA, crane operators were retrained to supervise operations remotely. This improved productivity and made jobs less physically demanding. This shows that workers can adapt to new technologies.

Even so, change can be difficult. Workers may need to learn new skills, and this can take time. Therefore, the government has introduced programs such as SkillsFuture to help workers upgrade their skills. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme provides Singaporeans aged 40 and above with credits and training allowances. This allows them to pursue further education.

For example, Vinoth Nanda Kumaran transitioned from a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer. He used SkillsFuture credits to study data engineering and later continued his studies. This shows that it is possible for workers to change careers.

The government will also work with unions and companies to redesign jobs. There will be more job matching services and career fairs. These initiatives aim to help workers find new opportunities. This is important because we want Singaporeans to benefit from AI.

At the same time, companies must also play their part. They should invest in training and support their workers. Educational institutions should also ensure that their courses are relevant. All stakeholders must work together.

Ultimately, Singapore must remain competitive. AI is one way to achieve this. By adopting AI, we can improve productivity and create better jobs. However, we must also ensure that workers are supported.

A dynamic economy is one that creates opportunities. Singapore has always relied on innovation and hard work. AI is another tool that we can use. If we adopt it carefully, we can continue to grow.

At the same time, we must remain united. Change can be challenging, but if we work together, we can overcome difficulties. Singapore has faced many challenges before, and we have succeeded because we adapted.

Artificial intelligence will continue to develop, and we must be prepared. By investing in our people and embracing new technologies, Singapore can remain competitive.

Thank you.
