Good evening, everyone.

It’s truly remarkable how quickly artificial intelligence has evolved. Just a few years ago, AI struggled with basic tasks—back in 2020, it couldn’t even solve simple math problems reliably. Yet by 2025, AI systems have advanced to the point where they can compete in the International Math Olympiad and achieve gold medal-level performance. Here in Singapore, AI is no longer a distant concept; it’s already woven into our daily lives. Whether it’s helping us plan trips by recommending destinations and crafting personalised itineraries, or assisting our civil service through call centres that transcribe conversations in all four official languages and generate summaries to help officers better serve the public—AI is quietly transforming how we live and work. Having seen how AI is already part of our lives, let’s explore how it can boost productivity and create new value across industries.

Singapore’s unique blend of cutting-edge technology and human expertise has made us a magnet for quality investments and good jobs. This isn’t just about large corporations; small and medium enterprises are also embracing AI to enhance their capabilities. Take Tuas Port, for example, where AI works alongside robotics and autonomous machines to streamline operations. At Changi Airport, researchers are exploring automation to improve efficiency. In the private sector, GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility uses AI to detect anomalies and assist human operators in making precise diagnoses, enhancing both speed and accuracy. Even SMEs like Q&M Dental harness AI to analyse dental X-rays more accurately, improving patient care. This widespread adoption shows that AI integration is accessible and beneficial across sectors of all sizes. While AI drives innovation and growth, it also raises concerns about jobs, which we must address thoughtfully.

It’s natural to worry about job displacement as technology advances. But history teaches us that while technological change disrupts the status quo, it also opens doors to higher-skilled and better-paying roles. In Singapore during the 1980s, our computerisation drive equipped workers with new skills that transformed our economy. Then in the 1990s, when the internet arrived, we embraced it wholeheartedly—propelling Singapore forward on the global stage. More recently at PSA, crane operators were retrained to remotely manage multiple cranes and vehicles simultaneously—a shift that not only improved productivity but also enhanced job quality by reducing physical strain and increasing operational control. These examples remind us that with the right support and upskilling opportunities, workers can successfully transition into new roles created by technological progress. Recognizing these lessons, Singapore is committed to prioritising its people in the AI era through targeted support and skills development.

To ensure no one is left behind in this transformation, the government has launched several initiatives designed to help Singaporeans adapt to an AI-driven workforce. We work closely with unions to redesign jobs so they remain meaningful while equipping workers with new skills needed for emerging roles. Expanded job matching services and career fairs connect Singaporeans with fresh opportunities across industries. One standout initiative is the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme: it offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above up to $4,000 in credits plus training allowances of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study—making mid-career training more accessible than ever before. A great example is Vinoth Nanda Kumaran, who transitioned from being a private hire driver into a Business Intelligence Developer by leveraging SkillsFuture credits alongside further studies at SUSS. These strong foundations give every Singaporean a fair chance to thrive amid rapid change. With these strong foundations, Singapore is poised to embrace AI for a dynamic and innovative future.

Looking ahead, our vision is clear: a future-ready Singapore where innovation drives growth—and where everyone benefits from good jobs and better lives. Embracing artificial intelligence thoughtfully and inclusively is key not only to enhancing productivity but also to securing our place on the global stage as a competitive economy built on ideas, innovation, and unity. Together, we will harness AI’s transformative potential while staying true to our commitment to uplift every member of society.

Thank you.
