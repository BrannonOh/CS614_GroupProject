Good evening, fellow Singaporeans.

Not long ago, artificial intelligence was a distant idea—something we only read about in science fiction or the news. Today, it’s part of our everyday lives here in Singapore. We use AI when we plan our holidays, getting personalised recommendations for destinations and customised itineraries. In our public service, AI is hard at work too. Our civil service call centres use it to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and produce summaries, so officers can spend more time helping you directly.

This rapid progress is striking. Back in 2020, AI struggled even with basic math problems. Fast forward to 2025, and AI can compete in the International Math Olympiad—and perform at a gold medal level. That’s how fast this technology is moving.

So AI is no longer something distant or theoretical. The real question for Singapore is how we harness it to strengthen our economy and stay competitive in a rapidly changing world.

Artificial intelligence will be a defining technology of our era. And Singapore must use it to raise productivity, boost competitiveness, and create better opportunities for all Singaporeans.

We’ve already seen how combining technology with human expertise attracts high-quality investments and creates good jobs here. AI isn’t just for big corporations; small and medium enterprises stand to gain too.

Take Tuas Port, where AI works alongside robotics and autonomous machines to streamline operations. Or Changi Airport, where automation powered by AI is being studied to improve efficiency. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility uses AI to detect anomalies in turbine parts and alert human experts for precise diagnosis—saving time and improving quality.

Even SMEs like Q&M Dental are using AI tools to analyse dental X-rays more accurately—spotting fillings, impacted teeth, and decay better than before.

These examples show the potential across sectors and company sizes.

But whenever new technology transforms industries, people naturally ask another important question: what will this mean for jobs?

It’s a fair concern. Technological change has always caused disruption. But history shows us something else: it also creates new opportunities and better jobs.

In the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with computer skills—and transformed our economy. When the internet arrived in the 1990s, we adopted it successfully and propelled the nation forward.

At PSA, crane operators who once worked on-site were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles at once. This boosted productivity while improving job quality.

Technological shifts are tough—but they don’t have to mean fewer jobs or lower standards of living. Instead, they can open doors to higher-skilled roles that pay better.

And just as we supported workers through past transformations, we must do the same again in the age of artificial intelligence.

Our approach will remain people-centred.

The government will work closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs so that workers are equipped with relevant skills for new roles created by AI adoption.

We’re expanding job matching services, holding more career fairs at tertiary institutions, and launching a new town-level job matching initiative led by Community Development Councils—to help Singaporeans find good jobs close to home.

The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme will grow too—offering more part-time training allowances and courses from industry leaders and private training providers.

This programme already provides Singaporeans aged 40 and above with $4,000 in SkillsFuture credits plus training allowances up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to two years.

Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran as an example—a 41-year-old in 2025 who transitioned from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits to get a diploma in data engineering—and then continuing his studies in Business Analytics at SUSS.

These efforts aren’t just about training or job placement—they’re about building an economy that remains dynamic, innovative, and full of opportunity for every Singaporean.

Ultimately, that’s what embracing AI is about: creating an economy that delivers good jobs and better lives for all of us.

By harnessing artificial intelligence effectively, Singapore stays globally competitive—not by chance but by design—with ideas that lead innovation and unity that strengthens us all.

This National Day Rally moment calls on us not just to reflect on how far we’ve come—but also on how we move forward together into this new era shaped by technology.

Thank you.
