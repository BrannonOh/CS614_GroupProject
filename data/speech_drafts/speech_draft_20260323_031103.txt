Good morning. Let’s start with a simple observation: AI has exploded from a niche tool to something you find in almost every corner of life here in Singapore. Just a few years ago, the idea of AI solving the toughest math problems at the International Mathematical Olympiad was pure science fiction. Yet in 2024, Google DeepMind’s AI models hit gold-medal standards there—a feat we couldn’t have imagined before. And it’s not just about math. A 2024 survey found that 55% of Singaporean travellers now use AI tools for planning their trips—getting personalized recommendations and tailored itineraries right at their fingertips. Even our civil service is on board, using AI in call centres to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and produce summaries, freeing officers to focus on what really matters: serving the public.

This rapid evolution—from barely usable tech to everyday assistant—is reshaping how we live and work in Singapore. That’s the reality today.

And it matters because this fast-changing AI landscape opens new doors for our economy and workforce.

Look around industries here. At Tuas Port, AI teams up with robotics and autonomous machines to speed up operations and cut errors. Over at Changi Airport, they’re exploring automation that could transform how things run day-to-day. GE Vernova uses AI right here to spot turbine problems early and help operators fix them quickly—keeping power flowing smoothly. And it’s not just the big players benefiting. SMEs like Q&M Dental are using AI to analyze dental X-rays more accurately, improving patient care.

Here’s the kicker: AI adoption among SMEs more than tripled—from 4.2% in 2023 to 14.5% this year. Big companies aren’t standing still either; their adoption jumped from 44% to 62.5%. This shows AI isn’t some exclusive club for giants—it’s lifting businesses across the board.

Singapore’s mix of smart technology and skilled people is drawing solid investments and creating real jobs—not just for big firms but for smaller ones too.

Does this mean jobs will disappear? That’s the question on many minds—and a fair one.

History tells us technological change shakes things up but doesn’t leave workers behind.

Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a national computerisation drive led by the National Computer Board—a clear move to boost our economic edge through tech. Then came public internet access in 1994, which took off fast: household computer ownership jumped from 36% in 1996 to 74% by 2005.

At PSA, crane operators didn’t lose out when automation arrived—they were retrained to remotely manage multiple cranes at once, boosting productivity and upgrading their job skills and pay.

Yes, technology disrupts jobs—but it also creates better ones that require higher skills and offer better pay.

And that lesson applies today as AI changes how we work.

We’re not repeating old mistakes this time. The government is working closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills for an AI-driven future.

Take the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme: Singaporeans aged 40 and above get a $4,000 credit top-up plus a training allowance of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study—real support for mid-career training.

Vinoth Nanda Kumaran is a great example—he moved from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer using SkillsFuture credits and further studies at SUSS. It’s proof that with the right help, career shifts are possible—even into tech-heavy roles.

NTUC’s Company Training Committee Grant has backed over 70 AI projects—including one with SBS Transit where technicians are learning to use AI for predictive bus maintenance—showing how job redesign goes hand-in-hand with upskilling.

Job matching services have expanded too, with career fairs helping Singaporeans connect directly with new opportunities across sectors.

These efforts ensure Singaporeans aren’t left behind—they’re ready and supported to thrive alongside AI.

So where does this leave us?

Singapore ranks second globally in AI readiness—and we’re backing that up by investing over S$1 billion into AI research through our National AI Strategy 2.0.

Why? Because staying competitive means embracing change head-on—not sitting on the sidelines hoping it passes us by.

A strong economy means good jobs and better lives for all Singaporeans—not just some of us.

We won’t settle for playing catch-up or following trends—we aim to lead through ideas, innovation, and unity.

The question I want you all to ask yourselves: Are we ready to take on this challenge together? Because if we do it right—thoughtfully and inclusively—AI won’t replace us; it will empower us all.

Thank you.
