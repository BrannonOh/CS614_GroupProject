Good morning, everyone.

Let’s start with a quick reality check. Back in 2020, AI couldn’t even solve basic math problems reliably. Fast forward to 2025, and AI is competing at the International Math Olympiad—performing at a gold medal level. That’s not just progress; it’s a leap. AI has gone from struggling with simple tasks to becoming a powerful tool embedded in everyday life here in Singapore.

Look around you. When you plan a trip, AI apps recommend destinations and create personalized itineraries tailored just for you. In our civil service, AI transcribes calls in all four official languages and summarizes them—freeing officers to focus on what really matters: serving the public. And in healthcare, AI is already at work in national screening programs for diabetes-related eye diseases across our public health system.

In just a few years, AI has transformed dramatically. It’s no longer some distant tech; it’s right here, integrated into daily activities that affect all of us. This rapid progress sets the stage for how AI can boost productivity and spark innovation across Singapore’s economy.

Now, what does this mean for our businesses and jobs?

AI is reshaping industries across the board. At Tuas Port, it teams up with robotics and autonomous machines to streamline operations. Changi Airport is exploring automation to stay ahead of global competition. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility uses AI to spot tiny faults in turbine parts—alerting human experts early so they can fix problems before they grow.

But it’s not just big players benefiting. SMEs like Q&M Dental use AI to analyze dental X-rays more accurately—catching fillings, impacted teeth, and decay that might have been missed before.

Why are global giants like BHP, Oracle, and PwC setting up AI hubs right here in Singapore? Because we offer a rare mix: cutting-edge technology plus skilled people ready to innovate together. This blend attracts investments that create good jobs—not just for large companies but also for small and medium businesses.

The benefits of AI are real—and they’re accessible to everyone from startups to multinationals.

But let’s be clear: while AI opens doors, it also threatens jobs. Displacement is real—and we cannot pretend otherwise.

History tells us how to handle this challenge.

Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that taught students and workers computer skills—transforming our economy completely. At PSA today, crane operators didn’t lose their jobs; instead, they were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles simultaneously—boosting productivity and making their work more skilled and satisfying.

Take this example: an Auntie Anne’s marketing employee used Workforce Singapore's Career Conversion Programmes (CCP) to retrain as a Customer Retention Specialist. She now analyzes data from new CRM systems—a clear upgrade from her previous role.

Technological change has always caused disruption first—but time after time, workers move into higher-skilled, better-paying roles. That’s our track record. Building on this history means the government is focused on helping Singaporeans adapt and thrive as AI changes work again.

And that help comes through concrete programs designed for real results.

The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above $4,000 in credits plus training allowances up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to two years. Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran: at 41 in 2025, he switched from private hire driver to Business Intelligence Developer by using SkillsFuture credits to get a diploma in data engineering—and he continues studying Business Analytics at SUSS.

NTUC has pushed over 70 projects focused on AI through its Company Training Committee Grant—helping about 10,000 workers gain new skills relevant today.

The government works closely with unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with fresh skills. Job matching services are expanding; career fairs connect people with opportunities; financial support backs mid-career training—all aimed at making sure no one gets left behind as AI reshapes work.

With these efforts underway, Singapore stands ready—not just to keep pace but to lead—in the AI era.

Where does this leave us?

Singapore's National AI Strategy 2.0 lays out a clear vision: “AI for the Public Good—for Singapore and the World.” It focuses on building peaks of excellence while empowering both people and businesses alike.

To back this up, we’re investing over S$1 billion in public AI research between 2025 and 2030—a serious commitment showing we mean business.

And here’s some proof: according to a 2025 Salesforce study, Singapore ranks second globally in AI readiness—and leads the world in AI governance.

A dynamic economy creates good jobs—and better lives—for all Singaporeans. Embracing AI isn’t optional if we want to stay competitive on the global stage; it’s essential.

So ask yourself: will we let others lead? Or will we take charge—through ideas, innovation, and unity?

Singapore aims not just to keep pace but to lead—and I believe we will.

Thank you.
