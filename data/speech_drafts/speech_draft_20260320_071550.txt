Good evening, fellow Singaporeans.

Artificial intelligence used to be something we read about in science fiction or tech magazines—far off and not really part of our daily lives. But look around today. AI is already here, woven into how we live and work in Singapore. When you plan a trip, AI can suggest destinations and even craft personalised itineraries just for you. In our civil service, AI listens in on calls across all four official languages, transcribing conversations and summarising them so officers can spend more time helping you directly. Think about this: in 2020, AI struggled with basic math problems. Fast forward to 2025, and it’s competing at the International Math Olympiad level—hitting gold medal standards. That’s how fast this technology is moving. At this National Day Rally—a moment for us to reflect on where we are and where we’re headed—AI is no longer a distant idea. It’s real, it matters, and it will shape the future for every Singaporean.

Artificial intelligence will define our era. If we want Singapore to stay competitive, raise productivity, and create better opportunities for all of us, we must harness AI now—not later.

AI is already transforming our economy and daily lives. What sets us apart is our ability to combine technology with human skills—that’s what draws high-quality investments and creates good jobs here. Take Tuas Port: AI works hand-in-hand with robotics and autonomous machines to run operations more efficiently and safely. At Changi Airport, AI is being tested for automation to streamline processes that keep us connected to the world. This isn’t just about big companies; small and medium enterprises are jumping on board too. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility uses AI to spot anomalies in turbine parts early—alerting human experts who then make precise diagnoses. It saves time and prevents bigger problems down the line. Even smaller outfits like Q&M Dental use AI to analyse dental X-rays more accurately—catching fillings, impacted teeth, or tooth decay that might otherwise be missed.

So yes, AI adoption isn’t just for the giants; SMEs can benefit just as much.

But here’s the hard question: when technology reshapes industries this fast, what happens to jobs?

This worry is real—and we can’t brush it aside. History tells us that technological change disrupts work but also opens doors to new opportunities and better-paying roles over time. Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that gave students and workers computer skills they needed—and that transformed our economy. Then came the internet in the 1990s; we didn’t hesitate—we adopted it fully—and that propelled us forward again.

Look at PSA: crane operators didn’t lose their jobs when automation arrived. Instead, they were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles at once—boosting productivity while improving their job quality.

Technological change shakes things up—but it also lets workers move into higher-skilled roles with better pay.

And just as we supported workers through those past shifts, we must do the same now—with artificial intelligence.

Our approach puts people first. The government is working closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs so workers can take on new tasks alongside AI tools—and get equipped with fresh skills needed for these changes. We’re expanding job matching services, running more career fairs at tertiary institutions, and launching a new town-level job matching initiative led by Community Development Councils—to connect Singaporeans with opportunities closer to home.

The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme is growing too: it now covers more part-time training allowances claims and includes more courses from industry leaders and private training providers.

For Singaporeans aged 40 and above, SkillsFuture credits provide up to $4,000 plus training allowances of up to $3,000 per month—for full-time study lasting up to two years.

Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran’s story: at 41 years old in 2025, he shifted from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits for a diploma in data engineering—and he’s still studying Business Analytics at SUSS today.

This isn’t just support on paper—it’s real help that changes lives by opening new career paths through job redesign and skills upgrading.

Building an economy that stays dynamic means creating good jobs that raise living standards across society. Embracing AI is not optional if we want Singapore to remain globally competitive—not by chasing trends blindly but by leading through ideas grounded in our strengths.

Singapore aims not just to keep pace but to stay ahead—through innovation backed by unity of purpose—and by focusing on what truly drives progress: people’s skills, adaptability, and opportunities.

Thank you.
