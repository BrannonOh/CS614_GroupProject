Good evening, everyone.

Let’s start with a simple observation: AI has come a long way. Not so long ago, these systems struggled with basic tasks. Today, they’re woven into the fabric of daily life here in Singapore. In 2025, AI models from Google and OpenAI scored gold medals in the International Mathematical Olympiad—yes, that competition known for expert-level human reasoning. Meanwhile, 55% of Singaporean travellers are already using AI to plan their trips, crafting personalised itineraries and finding new activities tailored just for them. And on the government front, GovTech’s ‘Transcribe’ platform is quietly working behind the scenes in call centres—transcribing conversations automatically in all four official languages and even Singlish—freeing up public officers to focus on what really matters: delivering better service. AI has transformed from limited capabilities to powerful tools integrated into Singaporean life. This rapid evolution sets the stage for how AI is boosting productivity and creating new value across industries in Singapore.

Now, let’s get to the heart of it: how AI is changing our economy and work. Look at GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility here—they use AI to spot anomalies in turbine components and alert human operators for precise diagnosis. It’s not just big players; SMEs like Q&M Dental are using AI to analyse dental X-rays with greater accuracy, detecting fillings, impacted teeth, and tooth decay more reliably than before. Then there’s Tuas Port—the world’s largest fully automated container-handling port—where AI and robotics are driving throughput and efficiency to new heights. Closer to home, Changi Airport is trialling AI-powered security systems that speed up checks by up to 50%, alongside autonomous baggage tractors that ramp up tarmac productivity. What does this tell us? Singapore’s unique mix of technology and human expertise attracts high-quality investments—and good jobs—to our shores. And it’s not just the giants benefiting; SMEs are catching on too, proving that AI adoption isn’t reserved for a few—it’s an opportunity for many. But while these benefits are clear, we can’t ignore the elephant in the room: concerns about job displacement that we must address thoughtfully.

History tells us something important about technological change—it disrupts but also creates opportunity. Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with computer skills, transforming our economy. At PSA today, crane operators have been retrained not just to operate cranes but to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles simultaneously—boosting productivity while improving job quality. And right now, the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) and the Institute of Banking and Finance (IBF) are teaming up with 11 leading financial institutions—including DBS, OCBC, and UOB—to pilot workforce transformation and upskilling initiatives aimed at preparing workers for an AI-enabled future. The point is clear: technological shifts have always caused disruption but have also enabled workers to move into higher-skilled, better-paying jobs. Building on this foundation, Singapore is committed to supporting workers through this AI transition with targeted initiatives.

That commitment isn’t just talk—it comes with real support on the ground. Take the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme: it offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above $4,000 in SkillsFuture credits plus training allowances of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to two years. Vinoth Nanda Kumaran is a great example—at 41 in 2025, he transitioned from being a private hire driver to becoming a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits to get a diploma in data engineering and continuing his studies in Business Analytics at SUSS. Beyond individuals like Vinoth, NTUC partners with companies through Company Training Committees (CTCs), providing grants covering up to 70% of costs related to AI adoption projects—including job redesign and skills training. The government also works closely with unions on this front. On top of training support, job matching services are expanding—with Community Development Councils (CDCs) now leading town-level initiatives that connect job seekers directly with vacancies close to home. This collaboration between government agencies, unions, employers—and you—is how we make sure no one gets left behind as jobs evolve.

With these strong supports in place, Singapore is ready to embrace AI for a dynamic and competitive future.

Why does this matter? Because according to a 2025 Salesforce study, Singapore ranks second globally in AI readiness—excelling not only in regulatory frameworks but also in actual AI adoption and investment environments. Under our National AI Strategy 2.0, we’re investing over S$1 billion over five years to boost public AI research and development capabilities further—strengthening our position as a global leader. A key goal? To more than triple our AI practitioner workforce—to 15,000 strong—and establish Singapore as a global hub for creators shaping tomorrow’s technologies.

At its core: a dynamic economy creates good jobs—and better lives—for all Singaporeans. Embracing AI supports our goal of staying globally competitive—not by chasing trends blindly but by leading through ideas, innovation—and unity.

Thank you.
