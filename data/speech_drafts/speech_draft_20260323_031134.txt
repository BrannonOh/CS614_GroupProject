Good morning. Let’s get straight to it: AI has exploded from a niche, experimental tool to something you see everywhere in Singapore today. Not long ago, the idea of AI cracking the toughest math problems at the International Mathematical Olympiad was pure fantasy. Yet in 2024, Google DeepMind’s AI models hit gold-medal standards there—a level we couldn’t have imagined before. But it’s not just about math. A 2024 survey found that 55% of Singaporean travellers now use AI tools for planning trips, getting personalized recommendations and itineraries right on their phones. And here’s something you might not know: Singapore’s civil service uses AI in call centres to transcribe conversations in all four official languages—English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil—and generate summaries. This frees officers to focus on what really matters: serving the public.

In just a few years, AI has gone from a futuristic idea to a practical tool woven into our daily lives. That rapid shift isn’t just impressive—it’s a game changer.

This fast evolution opens new doors for Singapore’s economy and workforce.

Look around our industries. At Tuas Port, AI works hand-in-hand with robotics and autonomous machines to speed up operations and cut errors. Over at Changi Airport, they’re studying automation closely to transform how things run day-to-day. GE Vernova uses AI right here in Singapore to detect turbine anomalies early and help human operators fix them fast—keeping power flowing smoothly. And it’s not just the big players benefiting. SMEs like Q&M Dental are using AI to analyze dental X-rays more accurately, improving patient care.

Here’s the hard truth: AI adoption among SMEs more than tripled—from 4.2% in 2023 to 14.5% this year. Big companies aren’t sitting still either; their adoption jumped from 44% to 62.5%. This isn’t some exclusive club for giants—it’s lifting businesses across the board.

Singapore’s mix of smart technology and skilled people is attracting solid investments and creating real jobs—not just for big firms but smaller ones too.

Now, let’s call this out: Yes, AI will disrupt jobs. Some roles will disappear or change beyond recognition.

But history tells us this isn’t new—and we don’t have to panic.

Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a national computerisation drive led by the National Computer Board—a deliberate move to boost our economic edge through technology. Then came public internet access in 1994, which took off fast: household computer ownership jumped from 36% in 1996 to 74% by 2005.

At PSA, when automation came in, crane operators didn’t get left behind—they were retrained to remotely manage multiple cranes at once, boosting productivity and upgrading their skills and pay.

Technological change shakes things up—but it also pushes workers into higher-skilled, better-paying jobs.

That lesson stands today as AI reshapes how we work.

We’re not repeating old mistakes this time around. The government is working closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills for an AI-driven future.

Take the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme: Singaporeans aged 40 and above get a $4,000 credit top-up plus a monthly training allowance of up to $3,000 for full-time study—real support for mid-career training.

Vinoth Nanda Kumaran is proof it works—he moved from private hire driver to Business Intelligence Developer using SkillsFuture credits and further studies at SUSS. Career shifts like his aren’t just possible; they’re happening now.

NTUC’s Company Training Committee Grant has backed over 70 AI projects—including one with SBS Transit where technicians are learning to use AI for predictive bus maintenance—showing how job redesign goes hand-in-hand with upskilling.

Job matching services have expanded too, with career fairs helping Singaporeans connect directly with new opportunities across sectors.

These efforts are clear: Singaporeans won’t be left behind—they’ll be ready and supported to thrive alongside AI.

So where does this leave us?

Let me be blunt: If you think Singapore can sit back while the world races ahead on AI—you’re wrong.

Singapore ranks second globally in AI readiness—and we’re backing that by investing over S$1 billion into AI research through our National AI Strategy 2.0.

Why? Because staying competitive means facing change head-on—not hoping it passes us by unnoticed or untouched.

A strong economy means good jobs and better lives for all Singaporeans—not just some of us.

We won’t settle for playing catch-up or following trends—we aim to set the pace through ideas and innovation backed by unity of purpose—not empty slogans.

The real question is this: Are we ready? Ready to meet this challenge head-on? Ready to shape an inclusive future where AI empowers all of us?

Because if we do this right—thoughtfully and inclusively—AI won’t replace us; it will make us stronger together.

Thank you.
