Have you ever wondered how secure Singapore’s energy supply really is amid the turmoil of global conflicts? It’s a pressing question, especially when you consider that Singapore depends heavily on imported energy, with over 95% of its electricity needs met through natural gas. This reliance makes us vulnerable. Global conflicts, such as those unfolding in the Middle East, pose real threats to the stability of supply and prices. When tensions rise in these regions, the ripple effects can be felt right here at home. Having established this vulnerability, let’s explore how Singapore remains resilient despite these challenges.

Singapore has taken significant steps to reduce these risks and maintain a steady energy supply. One key strategy is diversification—both in terms of energy sources and suppliers. Instead of relying on a single region or provider, Singapore imports natural gas from multiple countries, including Indonesia and Malaysia, through a combination of pipelines and liquefied natural gas shipments. This spread reduces our exposure to disruptions in any one area. Additionally, strong infrastructure and strategic reserves act as buffers against short-term shocks, ensuring that even if supply hiccups occur, our energy needs continue to be met without interruption. Building on this resilience, Singapore is also investing in a more secure energy future through innovation and sustainability.

Looking ahead, Singapore is actively planning for a future where renewable and low-carbon energy play a central role in our energy security. We are making strides to import renewable energy, such as solar power from regional grids, which will diversify our portfolio further while reducing carbon emissions. Moreover, ongoing efforts focus on developing low-carbon alternatives like hydrogen and enhancing overall energy efficiency across sectors. Ambitiously, Singapore aims to import up to 4 gigawatts of low-carbon electricity by 2035—this would cover about 30% of our projected demand. These initiatives not only reduce reliance on fossil fuels but also position us at the forefront of clean energy innovation. While technology and policy are vital, the role of individuals and society is equally important in securing our energy future.

Energy security isn’t just a challenge for the government or big corporations; it’s something every household and business can contribute to through conservation efforts. Since households account for a significant share of electricity consumption, even small changes in daily habits can collectively have a powerful impact on national resilience. By building awareness about responsible usage—turning off unused appliances, using energy-efficient devices, and adopting smarter consumption patterns—we strengthen our ability to weather external shocks together. Finally, let’s consider how Singapore balances energy security with sustainability and affordability through careful policy planning.

Singapore faces what is often called the “energy trilemma”: the need to balance reliable supply, environmental goals, and cost affordability all at once. Our policies must ensure that electricity remains available whenever we need it while also keeping prices reasonable for consumers and businesses alike. At the same time, we cannot lose sight of our commitment to sustainability—reducing emissions and protecting our environment for future generations. Through long-term planning that integrates these sometimes competing priorities, Singapore aims to stay secure even amid global uncertainties. In conclusion, Singapore’s multifaceted approach positions it well to face the energy challenges ahead.

Ultimately, securing Singapore’s energy future is a shared responsibility involving government bodies, industry players, and each one of us as citizens. By embracing diversification in our supplies, driving innovation in clean technologies, and committing to conservation at home and work, we are building an energy system that is not only resilient but also sustainable for generations to come. Together, we can ensure that Singapore continues to thrive with reliable power no matter what challenges arise on the global stage. Thank you.
