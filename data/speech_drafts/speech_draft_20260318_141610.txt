Fellow Singaporeans,

Not long ago, artificial intelligence — or AI — was a distant idea, something we read about in science fiction or saw in futuristic films. Today, it’s part of our everyday lives here in Singapore. Think about how many of us use AI when planning a holiday — it recommends destinations, creates personalized itineraries tailored just for you. In our public services, AI is already at work. Our civil service call centres use AI to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and generate summaries, so officers can focus on what matters: serving you better.

And the progress is staggering. Back in 2020, AI struggled to solve basic math problems reliably. Fast forward to 2025, and AI can compete at the International Math Olympiad — performing at a gold medal level. This isn’t science fiction anymore. It’s happening now.

So AI is no longer something distant or theoretical. The real question for Singapore is how we harness it to strengthen our economy and stay competitive in a rapidly changing world.

Singapore has always been good at combining technology with human expertise. That’s why we attract high-quality investments and create good jobs. This isn’t just about big corporations either. Small and medium enterprises are getting on board too.

Take Tuas Port — they’re using AI combined with robotics and autonomous machines to operate more efficiently. Changi Airport is studying automation possibilities powered by AI as well. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility here uses AI to spot anomalies in turbine parts and alert human operators for precise diagnosis. Even SMEs like Q&M Dental are using AI to analyze dental X-rays, helping dentists detect fillings, impacted teeth, and decay more accurately.

This kind of adoption raises productivity and creates real value across industries. But whenever new technology transforms industries, people naturally ask another important question: what will this mean for jobs?

It’s a fair concern. Technological change has always disrupted jobs before — but it has also opened doors to better ones.

Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with computer skills and transformed our economy. When the internet arrived in the 1990s, we didn’t hesitate; we adopted it quickly and propelled the nation forward.

Look at PSA today: crane operators were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles simultaneously. This not only boosted productivity but also improved job quality — workers moved into higher-skilled roles.

We’ve faced these challenges before — and come out stronger.

And just as we supported workers through past transformations, we must do the same again in the age of artificial intelligence.

Our approach will remain people-centred. The government is working closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills for this new era.

We’re expanding efforts to help Singaporeans access jobs—through enhanced job matching services, more career fairs at tertiary institutions, and a new town-level job matching initiative led by Community Development Councils.

The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme will grow too. More part-time training allowance claims will be included along with courses from industry leaders and private training providers.

For those aged 40 and above, SkillsFuture provides $4,000 in credits plus training allowances up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to two years.

Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran as an example: at 41 years old in 2025, he transitioned from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits for a diploma in data engineering—and continued his studies in Business Analytics at SUSS.

Ultimately, these efforts are about something bigger: building an economy that remains dynamic, innovative, and full of opportunity for every Singaporean.

A dynamic economy creates good jobs and better lives for all of us. Embracing AI supports Singapore’s goal of staying globally competitive—not by chasing trends blindly—but by leading through ideas, innovation, and unity.

We face no shortage of challenges ahead. But with clear-eyed pragmatism—and by putting people first—we can harness this defining technology of our era for the benefit of every Singaporean today and tomorrow.

Thank you.
