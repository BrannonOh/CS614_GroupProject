Fellow Singaporeans,

Not long ago, artificial intelligence—AI—was a distant idea, something we read about in science fiction or saw on the big screen. Today, it’s part of everyday life here in Singapore. Many of us use AI when planning our travels—it recommends destinations and generates personalized itineraries tailored to our preferences. In our public services, AI is already hard at work. Our civil service call centres use AI to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and produce summaries, allowing officers to focus on serving you better.

The pace of AI’s progress is striking. In 2020, AI struggled to solve basic math problems reliably. By 2025, it can compete at the International Math Olympiad—and perform at a gold medal level. This is not some far-off future; it’s happening now.

Artificial intelligence will be a defining technology of our era. Singapore must harness it to raise productivity, strengthen our competitiveness, and create better opportunities for all Singaporeans.

So AI is no longer something distant or theoretical. The real question for Singapore is how we harness it to strengthen our economy and stay competitive in a rapidly changing world.

Singapore has always excelled at combining technology with human expertise. This ability attracts high-quality investments and creates good jobs for our people. And this isn’t just about large corporations—small and medium enterprises are reaping the benefits too.

At Tuas Port, AI is being combined with robotics and autonomous machines to boost efficiency. Changi Airport is actively studying how AI-powered automation can improve operations. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility here uses AI to detect anomalies in turbine components and alert human operators for precise diagnosis. Even SMEs like Q&M Dental use AI to analyse dental X-rays more accurately—helping dentists detect fillings, impacted teeth, and tooth decay.

These examples show how AI adoption raises productivity and creates real value across industries. But whenever new technology reshapes industries, people naturally ask: what will this mean for jobs?

It’s a valid concern—and one we’ve faced before.

Technological change has always disrupted jobs initially but also opened doors to better ones.

In the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with computer skills and transformed the economy. When the internet arrived in the 1990s, we adopted it swiftly—and used it as a foundation for growth.

Take PSA today: crane operators were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles at once. This boosted productivity and improved job quality—workers moved into higher-skilled roles.

We’ve been through this cycle before—and came out stronger.

Just as we supported workers through past transformations, we must do the same again in the age of artificial intelligence.

Our approach will remain people-centred. The government commits to working closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills for this new era.

We’re expanding efforts to help Singaporeans access jobs—including enhanced job matching services, more career fairs at tertiary institutions, and a new town-level job matching initiative led by Community Development Councils (CDCs).

The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme will also expand—covering more part-time training allowance claims and adding courses from industry leaders and private training providers.

For Singaporeans aged 40 and above, SkillsFuture provides $4,000 in credits plus training allowances up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to 24 months.

Consider Vinoth Nanda Kumaran: at 41 years old in 2025, he transitioned from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits for a diploma in data engineering—and continued his studies in Business Analytics at SUSS.

Ultimately, these efforts are about building an economy that remains dynamic, innovative, and full of opportunity for every Singaporean.

A dynamic economy is one that creates good jobs and better lives for all of us. Embracing AI supports Singapore’s goal of staying globally competitive—not by chasing trends blindly—but by leading through ideas, innovation, and shared purpose.

We face tough challenges ahead. But if we face them head-on—with clear focus on people first—we can harness this defining technology of our era for every Singaporean today and tomorrow.

Thank you.
