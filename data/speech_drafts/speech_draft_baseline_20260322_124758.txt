Just a few years ago, artificial intelligence was struggling with tasks as simple as primary school math. Today, the most advanced AI models can solve problems at the level of the International Mathematical Olympiad, showcasing an extraordinary leap in reasoning capabilities. This rapid progress means that AI is no longer a distant, futuristic idea—it is very much here and now. In Singapore, AI has become an integral part of our daily lives and public services. For example, our public service agencies use AI to transcribe and summarize calls in all four official languages, freeing up officers to focus on delivering better support to citizens. In our hospitals, AI-powered tools assist doctors by reading medical scans like mammograms, enabling earlier and more accurate diagnoses. This rapid evolution of AI opens new opportunities for Singapore to boost productivity and create value across sectors.

Singapore’s approach to AI is clear: we are not looking to replace humans with machines but to augment human expertise with intelligent tools. This synergy enhances capabilities and attracts high-value investments into our economy. Importantly, the benefits of AI-driven productivity are not limited to large corporations; they extend deeply into Small and Medium Enterprises across various industries. Take Tuas Port, for instance—one of the world’s most advanced ports—where AI coordinates a fleet of autonomous vehicles and yard cranes, significantly improving efficiency and cargo throughput. In advanced manufacturing, GE Vernova employs AI to analyze images of turbine blades, helping technicians detect microscopic defects invisible to the naked eye, thereby ensuring higher safety and quality standards. Even local SMEs like Q&M Dental Group have embraced AI systems that analyze dental X-rays with greater precision, allowing dentists to identify cavities and other issues more accurately for better patient care. While these advancements drive growth and innovation, they also raise concerns about job displacement—concerns we must address thoughtfully.

History teaches us that every technological shift initially causes anxiety about jobs but ultimately creates new opportunities that are often higher-value and previously unimaginable. Singapore has a strong track record in navigating these transitions by investing in its people and turning potential disruption into competitive advantage. During the computerisation wave of the 1980s, we launched a national effort to train an entire generation—from students to office workers—building a tech-savvy workforce ready for change. When the internet arrived, we built one of the world’s first nationwide broadband networks, sparking new industries like e-commerce and digital media that created thousands of new jobs. Even here at our port, crane operators were retrained as remote operations specialists who manage multiple automated cranes from high-tech control centers—resulting in better pay and improved working conditions. Building on this history, Singapore is now prioritising its people once again to ensure they thrive in the era of AI.

To support this vision, Singapore’s tripartite partnership among government, unions, and employers is actively redesigning jobs so that AI tools serve workers rather than replace them. Lifelong learning is becoming a practical reality through substantial subsidies and support designed especially for mid-career workers who want to upskill without financial strain. Personalized career support is also being scaled up—with job matching services and career coaching increasingly accessible right at the community level. For example, the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above a $4,000 credit top-up plus a monthly training allowance—making it feasible for many to take time off work for full-time courses in areas like AI and data analytics. One inspiring story is that of Vinoth Nanda Kumaran, who used these programs to transition from being a private-hire driver into a Business Intelligence Developer—a clear testament that pathways into tech careers are open to everyone regardless of where they start from. These initiatives pave the way for a future where all Singaporeans can confidently embrace AI and innovation.

Our goal goes beyond economic growth—we strive for inclusive growth where productivity gains driven by AI translate into better jobs, higher wages, and an improved quality of life for every Singaporean. In a world rapidly reshaped by artificial intelligence, standing still is not an option. Proactively embracing this technology is how we secure our nation’s relevance and prosperity for generations ahead. But our greatest asset isn’t technology itself—it’s our people. By uniting our ideas, spirit of innovation, and collective willpower, we can build a future where every Singaporean thrives alongside AI.

Thank you.
