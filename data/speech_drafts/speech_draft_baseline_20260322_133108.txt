It’s truly remarkable how fast artificial intelligence has evolved. Not too long ago, in 2020, AI struggled to solve basic math problems reliably. Fast forward just five years, and AI systems are now competing in the International Math Olympiad — even achieving gold medal-level performance. This rapid progress isn’t just impressive on paper; it’s something we experience every day here in Singapore. AI is already embedded in our daily lives — helping us plan our travels by recommending destinations and generating personalised itineraries tailored just for us. It’s also at work behind the scenes in our public services. For instance, Singapore’s civil service uses AI in agency call centres to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and produce summaries that allow officers to focus more effectively on serving the public.

Having seen how AI is already part of our lives, let's explore how it boosts productivity and creates new value across industries.

Singapore’s unique blend of cutting-edge technology and human expertise has made it a magnet for quality investments and good jobs. But AI isn’t just for large corporations; it’s accessible and beneficial to companies of all sizes, including small and medium enterprises. Take Tuas Port, where AI works hand-in-hand with robotics and autonomous machines to streamline operations. Similarly, Changi Airport is actively studying automation technologies powered by AI to enhance efficiency. On the industrial front, GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility leverages AI to detect anomalies early and assist human operators with precise diagnostics, improving both speed and accuracy. Even SMEs like Q&M Dental are harnessing AI — using it to analyze dental X-rays more accurately for better detection of dental issues.

While these advancements drive growth and innovation, they also raise important concerns about jobs — concerns we must address thoughtfully.

History shows us that technological change often disrupts the status quo but ultimately creates opportunities for workers to move into higher-skilled, better-paying roles. In the 1980s, Singapore embarked on a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with essential computer skills, transforming our economy in the process. When the internet arrived in the 1990s, Singapore embraced it wholeheartedly, propelling national progress forward at a rapid pace. More recently, at PSA Singapore Terminals, crane operators were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles simultaneously — a shift that not only boosted productivity but also improved job quality by reducing physical strain.

Building on this legacy, Singapore is prioritising its people to thrive in the AI era through targeted support and skills development.

The government is working closely with unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills needed for tomorrow’s economy. Efforts include expanded job matching services and career fairs designed to connect Singaporeans with emerging opportunities across industries. At the community level, town initiatives are helping residents discover pathways to meaningful employment. A key pillar of this support is the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme, which is expanding to offer more training courses from industry leaders. This programme provides Singaporeans aged 40 and above with up to $4,000 in credits as well as training allowances of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study. One inspiring example is Vinoth Nanda Kumaran — who transitioned from working as a private hire driver to becoming a Business Intelligence Developer by leveraging SkillsFuture credits along with further studies at SUSS.

With these foundations firmly in place, Singapore can confidently embrace AI to build a dynamic, competitive future for all.

A dynamic economy doesn’t just create wealth; it creates good jobs and better lives for every Singaporean. Embracing artificial intelligence thoughtfully and inclusively is key to maintaining our global competitiveness in this fast-changing world. By leading through ideas, innovation, and unity, Singapore will continue shaping a future where technology empowers everyone — ensuring that no one is left behind.
