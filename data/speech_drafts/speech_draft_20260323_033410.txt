Let’s cut to the chase. Just a few months ago, in early 2024, AI models were stuck on grade-school math problems. Now? They’re winning gold medals at the International Mathematical Olympiad. That’s not a slow crawl—it’s a rocket launch. Here in Singapore, AI isn’t some far-off future. It’s already woven into our daily lives. Our civil service call centres use AI to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and generate summaries for officers. And it’s not just government work—over half of Singaporean travellers, 55%, are ready to use AI tools when planning their trips, according to a Booking.com study. In just a few years, AI has shifted from limited capabilities to top-tier performance, embedded deeply in how we live and work. This rapid evolution and integration of AI opens new possibilities for Singapore’s industries and workforce, which we’ll dive into next.

Here’s the big idea: by embracing artificial intelligence thoughtfully and inclusively, Singapore can boost productivity, create new opportunities, and secure its future competitiveness—all while putting the welfare of our people first.

So what does this look like on the ground? Singapore is blending technology with human know-how like no other place. Take Tuas Port: AI teams up with robotics and autonomous machines to run operations more efficiently. At Changi Airport, they’re actively studying how AI can automate key tasks to keep things moving smoothly. GE Vernova uses AI here in Singapore to spot turbine problems early and assist human operators in making quicker decisions—AI is a partner, not a replacement. And it’s not just big players—SMEs like Q&M Dental rely on AI to analyze dental X-rays more accurately than ever before, improving diagnoses and care. This mix of tech and talent is attracting investments and creating quality jobs across the board—from large companies down to small businesses. The government isn’t sitting on the sidelines either; it’s driving AI adoption through strategic programmes and working closely with global tech leaders to transform industries right here at home. The result? Higher productivity and stronger competitiveness for Singapore on the global stage. But let me be clear: while AI fuels innovation and growth, it also sparks real worries about job losses—and we can’t shy away from that.

Let’s be blunt: technological change shakes things up—and that can be unsettling or even scary. But history tells us it doesn’t have to mean losing out. Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a computerisation drive that equipped workers with new skills and transformed our economy from manufacturing-heavy to knowledge-driven growth. At PSA, crane operators didn’t lose their jobs—they were retrained to remotely manage multiple cranes simultaneously, boosting both productivity and job quality. The 1980s Civil Service Computerisation Programme automated routine tasks but also laid the foundation for e-services like TradeNet that businesses rely on today for smoother trade processing. Disruption happens—but it opens doors too. The focus now isn’t replacing jobs; it’s redesigning them—using AI to augment human capabilities and create higher-value roles that pay better and demand more skill. That means we all need a mindset shift: continuous learning and upskilling aren’t optional anymore—they’re essential if we want to stay relevant and grab the opportunities AI creates. Recognizing these lessons, Singapore is committed to making sure our people are prepared—and prioritized—in this transition.

How exactly are we doing that? With real action—not empty talk. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above a $4,000 credit top-up plus a training allowance of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study—a serious boost for mid-career training. Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran as an example: he went from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer by using SkillsFuture credits along with further studies at SUSS—a clear proof these programmes work on the ground. Meanwhile, in fiscal year 2024/2025 alone, Workforce Singapore (WSG) and its partners placed more than 56,000 locals into jobs through expanded career matching services and targeted job fairs designed specifically for Singaporeans navigating this shifting landscape. The government is also working hand-in-hand with unions—to redesign jobs so workers get equipped with the right skills for tomorrow’s economy. These efforts put Singaporeans front and center in our nation’s AI-driven future.

Looking ahead—Singapore isn’t just adapting; we’re setting bold targets under our National AI Strategy 2.0 aiming for AI to contribute about US$29.6 billion to our economy by 2030—that’s no small change at all. A recent Salesforce study ranked us second globally—and first in Asia-Pacific—for AI readiness, putting us ahead of many bigger economies out there already grappling with this shift. And in 2025 alone, more than 105,000 people took up SkillsFuture-supported AI-related training courses—a clear sign we’re building a workforce ready for this new era.

A dynamic economy creates good jobs—and real opportunities—for all of us here in Singapore. Embracing AI isn’t optional if we want to keep our edge globally—it’s essential. But beyond technology or dollars lies what truly makes us strong: sharp ideas, relentless innovation—and yes—a willingness to challenge ourselves as one nation moving forward together.

Thank you.
