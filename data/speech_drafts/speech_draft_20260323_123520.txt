Look around. AI isn’t just a future idea—it’s already woven into daily life here in Singapore. When you plan a trip, AI recommends destinations and creates personalised itineraries for you. It helps you book flights, navigate government services online, and handles countless small tasks behind the scenes without you even noticing. This seamless integration signals that we’ve stepped into a new era where AI is not just a tool but a core part of how we live and work.

This growing presence of AI sets the stage for how it can further boost Singapore’s economy and competitiveness.

Across sectors, adopting AI means more than just new technology—it means higher productivity and fresh economic value. AI automates routine work, letting people focus on more complex tasks. It processes data faster and more accurately than humans can, improving decisions across industries. This leads to smarter operations, higher output, and new opportunities for innovation. In practical terms, AI helps businesses produce better products and services with fewer mistakes and less waste. The result is a stronger economy that creates value in ways we haven’t seen before.

To understand this better, let’s look at concrete examples of AI applications in Singapore’s industries.

At Tuas Port, AI works alongside robotics and autonomous machines to streamline huge logistics operations—making the port more efficient and competitive globally. Changi Airport is exploring AI-driven automation to improve both safety and operational efficiency. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility uses AI to spot anomalies in turbine parts early on; this lets human operators diagnose issues precisely before they become costly repairs, saving time and money. Even smaller players like Q&M Dental use AI to analyse dental X-rays more accurately—helping detect fillings, impacted teeth, or decay sooner—which improves patient care while boosting the clinic’s efficiency.

While these benefits are clear, we cannot ignore concerns about job displacement.

Technological change has always disrupted jobs—but history shows it also opens doors to better opportunities. When PSA automated its ports, crane operators didn’t lose their jobs; instead, they were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles at once. This shift didn’t just maintain employment—it boosted productivity significantly and improved job quality by moving workers into higher-skilled roles with better pay.

Building on these lessons, the government is actively supporting Singaporeans through this AI-driven transition.

We’re making sure no one gets left behind. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above up to $4,000 in SkillsFuture credits plus training allowances of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to 24 months. For example, take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran: at 41 in 2025, he shifted from being a private hire driver to becoming a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits to earn a diploma in data engineering—and he continued his studies in Business Analytics at SUSS. Beyond training support, we’ve expanded job matching services with more career fairs at tertiary institutions and launched new town-level job matching initiatives led by Community Development Councils. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme will also expand to cover more part-time training allowance claims and include additional courses from industry leaders and private training providers.

With these supports in place, Singapore is positioned not just to adopt AI—but to do so thoughtfully and inclusively—so everyone can benefit from the changes ahead.

A dynamic economy is one that creates good jobs and better lives for all Singaporeans—not just some of us. Embracing AI isn’t about chasing trends; it’s about practical steps—upgrading skills, improving industries, managing transitions—that keep Singapore competitive on the global stage. Our goal remains clear: stay ahead through ideas, innovation—and unity among all Singaporeans.

Thank you.
