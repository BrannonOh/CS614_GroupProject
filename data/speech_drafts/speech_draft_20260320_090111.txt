Good evening, everyone.

Let me start with a simple story. Not long ago, I was planning a trip overseas using an AI travel planner. It sorted flights, hotels, and even suggested local eateries in seconds. Or take a language translation app—so good now it feels like having a personal interpreter right in your pocket. We barely notice these tools anymore because they’ve quietly become part of our daily lives. But here’s the thing: this is just the start of a massive wave of technological change that’s about to reshape everything.

To secure its future and stay competitive globally, Singapore must fully embrace Artificial Intelligence across our economy and society. We need to turn challenges like job displacement into real opportunities for growth, innovation, and better lives for all Singaporeans.

We are entering a new era with AI—and fast. Back in 2020, AI struggled to solve basic math problems reliably. By 2024, Google DeepMind’s AlphaGeometry system was solving problems at gold-medal level at the International Mathematical Olympiad—a leap from niche tech to mainstream power.

This isn’t some distant future. A recent survey found that 8 in 10 Singaporeans are already using AI tools for personal tasks. Even within our civil service, AI transcribes calls in all four official languages and generates summaries so officers can focus on serving you instead of drowning in paperwork.

The rapid rise of Generative AI marks a pivotal shift—it’s not just changing daily life; it’s fundamentally reshaping the global economy.

But this new era isn’t only about personal convenience; it’s a powerful engine driving our national economy forward. So, how do we harness it?

Singapore is already putting AI to work across many sectors. At Tuas Port, AI teams up with robotics and autonomous machines to handle massive cargo volumes efficiently—keeping our port competitive globally. Changi Airport is actively studying automation to boost operations and improve passenger experience.

Look at GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility here: they use AI to spot anomalies in turbine parts early and alert human experts for precise fixes—saving time and costs while maintaining quality. Even small businesses benefit: Q&M Dental uses AI to analyze dental X-rays more accurately, detecting fillings, impacted teeth, and decay earlier than before.

Big players like Oracle, PwC, and Grab have announced plans to invest billions of dollars into AI hubs right here in Singapore. Why? Because we know how to combine technology with human expertise—and that combination attracts top-tier investments while creating good jobs for Singaporeans.

And don’t assume this is only for large companies; small and medium enterprises stand to gain significantly as well.

But as we see these productivity leaps, there’s a question on many minds: what does this mean for jobs?

It’s a valid concern—and one we must face head-on.

History shows us that technological change disrupts jobs but also creates new opportunities if we adapt smartly.

In the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with computer skills—transforming our economy from the ground up. When the internet arrived in the 1990s, we adopted it successfully and propelled ourselves forward into the digital age.

At PSA, crane operators didn’t lose their jobs—they were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles simultaneously. This boosted productivity while improving job quality—a clear win-win.

That said, jobs involving routine tasks—in administration, customer service, and logistics—are most exposed to disruption by AI automation.

But at the same time, demand is growing rapidly for new roles like AI Engineers, Machine Learning Specialists, and Data Scientists—jobs that require higher skills and offer better pay.

Technological change has always been disruptive but also opened doors for workers to move into higher-skilled roles with better prospects—and AI will be no different if we manage it well.

History shows we can navigate these shifts successfully—but only if we act deliberately. No one should be left behind in this transition.

Even as we embrace AI and technology, Singaporeans remain our key priority.

The government is working closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills through real collaboration on transformation projects.

Programs like the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme provide Singaporeans aged 40 and above with $4,000 worth of SkillsFuture credits plus training allowances for full-time study—and this program will expand further to include more part-time training allowance claims as well as more courses from industry leaders and private training providers.

Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran as an example—at 41 years old in 2025, he shifted from being a private hire driver to becoming a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits for a diploma in data engineering and continuing studies at SUSS in Business Analytics.

Through NTUC’s Company Training Committees (CTCs), unions and management collaborate closely on transformation projects—the number of AI-related projects more than doubled in 2025 compared to 2024 alone.

Efforts also include expanded job matching services, more career fairs at tertiary institutions, plus new town-level job matching initiatives led by Community Development Councils—all aimed squarely at helping Singaporeans find good jobs amid change.

These individual efforts add up—they’re part of a much larger national strategy designed around people first.

Make no mistake: embracing AI fits squarely within Singapore's broader goal of building a dynamic economy that creates good jobs and better lives for all citizens while keeping us competitive on the global stage.

A recent Salesforce study ranked Singapore second worldwide in AI readiness—not just because of technology but thanks specifically to our strong regulatory foundation alongside robust digital infrastructure—which positions us as leaders going forward.

And it shows up clearly in paychecks too: median annual salaries for AI professionals hit S$133,300 in 2025—well above the national median—proving high-value jobs are being created right now on our shores.

A dynamic economy means opportunity for everyone—a future powered by ideas, innovation—and unity among us all as Singaporeans determined not just to keep up but to lead ahead into tomorrow.

Thank you.
