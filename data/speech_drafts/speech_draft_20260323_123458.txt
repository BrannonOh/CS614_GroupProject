Good morning.

Look around. AI isn’t some distant idea anymore. It’s already part of daily life in Singapore. When you plan a trip, AI recommends destinations and even crafts personalised itineraries for you. It helps book flights, navigate government services online, and smooths out countless small tasks behind the scenes. We’re entering a new era—one where AI is not just a tool but a fundamental part of how we live and work.

This growing presence of AI sets the stage for how it can further boost Singapore’s economy and competitiveness.

AI adoption across sectors raises productivity in clear ways. It automates routine tasks, freeing up people to focus on higher-value work. It improves decision-making by analysing data faster and more accurately than humans alone. This means businesses can deliver better products and services with fewer errors and less waste. The result? New economic value created through smarter operations, higher output, and fresh opportunities for innovation. In short, AI helps us work smarter—not just harder—and that lifts the entire economy.

To understand this better, let’s look at concrete examples of AI applications in Singapore’s industries.

At Tuas Port, AI combines with robotics and autonomous machines to streamline massive logistics operations. Changi Airport is studying automation powered by AI to boost efficiency and safety. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility uses AI to detect anomalies in turbine components early—alerting human operators who then diagnose issues precisely before they become costly problems. Even SMEs like Q&M Dental rely on AI to analyse dental X-rays more accurately, spotting fillings, impacted teeth, and decay that might otherwise go unnoticed.

While these benefits are real, we cannot ignore concerns about job displacement.

Technological change disrupts jobs—but history shows disruption also creates better opportunities. When PSA automated its ports, crane operators didn’t lose their jobs; they were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles simultaneously. This shift increased productivity and moved workers into higher-skilled roles with better pay and improved job quality.

Building on these lessons, the government is actively supporting Singaporeans through this AI-driven transition.

We’re not leaving anyone behind. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above up to $4,000 in SkillsFuture credits plus training allowances of up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to 24 months. Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran: at 41 in 2025, he shifted from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits to earn a diploma in data engineering—and he continued studying Business Analytics at SUSS. Beyond training support, we’ve expanded job matching services with more career fairs at tertiary institutions and launched new town-level job matching initiatives led by Community Development Councils. The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme will also grow to cover more part-time training allowances and courses from industry leaders and private providers.

With these supports in place, Singapore is ready to embrace AI for a dynamic and competitive future.

A dynamic economy creates good jobs and better lives for all Singaporeans—not just some of us. Embracing AI is essential if we want to maintain our global competitiveness. This isn’t about chasing trends; it’s about practical steps—adapting skills, upgrading industries, managing transitions—that keep Singapore ahead through clear-eyed planning and collective effort.

Thank you.
