Good evening, everyone.

It’s remarkable to reflect on how quickly artificial intelligence has evolved. Just a few years ago, AI struggled with basic reasoning tasks. Today, we have models like AlphaGeometry that can solve complex International Mathematical Olympiad problems at a gold-medal standard. This exponential pace of development has transformed AI from a niche technology into a powerful tool capable of superhuman performance in complex domains.

But beyond these impressive feats, AI is no longer just a novelty. It has quietly become an invisible engine powering everyday convenience and critical public services across Singapore. For example, in our polyclinics, AI-powered tools screen for diabetic retinopathy—a leading cause of blindness—enabling earlier detection and treatment for thousands of patients. Meanwhile, Singapore’s public service harnesses AI to transcribe and summarize call centre conversations in all four official languages, improving efficiency and service quality for citizens.

Having seen how AI is already part of our lives, let’s explore how it boosts productivity and creates new value across industries.

AI acts as a “co-pilot” for human workers, augmenting their skills and enabling new levels of precision and productivity that attract high-value investments. This democratization of AI tools means that Singapore’s small and medium enterprises can compete on a global scale, driving innovation from the ground up.

Take Tuas Port, for instance. There, AI coordinates a fleet of automated guided vehicles and yard cranes, creating one of the world’s most efficient and advanced container terminals. At GE Vernova’s advanced repair facility, AI-powered systems scan complex turbine blades to identify microscopic defects invisible to the human eye—boosting both safety and reliability. Even local SMEs like Q&M Dental Group use AI to analyze X-rays with over 90% accuracy, helping dentists detect cavities and other issues earlier for better patient outcomes.

While AI drives growth, concerns about job displacement naturally arise; let’s address these concerns and highlight the opportunities available for workers.

History shows us that Singapore has consistently turned technological disruption into an opportunity for workforce transformation—leading to better jobs and higher value creation. The key is not to resist technology but to proactively redesign jobs and upskill our people to master new tools.

During the 1980s national computerisation drive, thousands of clerks and administrators were retrained as proficient IT users—forming the backbone of our modern service economy. When PSA automated its port operations, crane operators were not replaced; instead, they were upskilled to become remote operations specialists who manage multiple automated cranes from control centres. This shift resulted in higher pay and a safer working environment.

Building on this foundation, the government is prioritizing Singaporeans by supporting job redesign and skills upgrading so everyone can thrive in the AI era.

Singapore is implementing a comprehensive national strategy built on three pillars: job redesign, skills empowerment, and career support. We are making significant investments in mid-career workers because their experience is a valuable asset when combined with new AI-related skills.

For example, the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme provides Singaporeans aged 40 and above with a $4,000 credit top-up plus substantial training allowances—enabling them to pursue full-time industry-relevant diplomas. This support has been life-changing: Vinoth Nanda Kumaran used these schemes to transition from being a private-hire driver to becoming a Business Intelligence Developer—a clear demonstration that switching careers into tech is achievable.

Moreover, over 100 Job Transformation Maps have been co-developed with industries. These maps outline specific skills needed for future roles and guide our training programs so they meet real-world demand effectively.

With these supports in place, Singapore can confidently embrace AI to build a dynamic and competitive future for all.

Our goal is not just to adopt AI but to master it—to create a future where technology serves human ambition and opens opportunities for every Singaporean to achieve a better life. By choosing collaboration over conflict and continuous learning over complacency, we will secure not only our global competitiveness but also build a more inclusive and dynamic society.

Let us move forward together—with confidence and unity—to write the next chapter of the Singapore story: one powered by human ingenuity and amplified by artificial intelligence.

Thank you.
