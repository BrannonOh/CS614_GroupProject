Good evening, fellow Singaporeans.

Not long ago, artificial intelligence—AI—was something we only heard about in science fiction or distant tech labs. Today, it’s right here with us, woven into the fabric of our daily lives. Think about how many of us use AI just to plan a holiday: it suggests destinations, crafts personalized itineraries. Even in government services, AI is hard at work. Our civil service call centres use AI to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and generate summaries, freeing officers to focus on helping you better. And consider this: in 2020, AI struggled with basic math problems. Fast forward to 2025, and it can compete—and win gold-level awards—at the International Math Olympiad. That’s how fast this technology is advancing.

So AI is no longer something distant or theoretical. The real question for Singapore is how we harness it to strengthen our economy and stay competitive in a rapidly changing world.

Singapore has always been good at combining technology with human expertise. This mix attracts high-quality investments and creates good jobs for our people. Take Tuas Port: AI works alongside robotics and autonomous machines to streamline operations. At Changi Airport, automation powered by AI is being studied to improve efficiency. In the private sector, companies like GE Vernova use AI to spot anomalies in turbine components before human operators step in for precise repairs. And it’s not just big players benefiting—small and medium enterprises are on board too. Q&M Dental uses AI to analyze dental X-rays more accurately, detecting fillings, impacted teeth, and decay that might be missed otherwise.

AI adoption isn’t reserved for the giants; it’s an opportunity for all businesses here.

But whenever new technology transforms industries, people naturally ask another important question: what will this mean for jobs?

We’ve faced this before—and yes, technological change can disrupt lives. But history shows us something else: it also creates new opportunities and better jobs.

Back in the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that equipped students and workers with computer skills—a bold move that transformed our economy. When the internet arrived in the 1990s, we didn’t hesitate; we adopted it quickly and used it as a springboard for growth.

Look at PSA today: crane operators who once worked on-site are now retrained to supervise multiple cranes remotely. This shift not only boosts productivity but improves job quality by reducing physical strain and increasing oversight responsibilities.

Change is never easy—but it can lead us forward.

And just as we supported workers through past transformations, we must do the same again in the age of artificial intelligence.

Our approach will be people-centred from start to finish.

The government is working closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with new skills that match evolving demands. We’re expanding job matching services—more career fairs at tertiary institutions and a new town-level job matching initiative led by Community Development Councils—to help Singaporeans find opportunities where they live.

SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme will grow too: more part-time training allowances and a wider range of courses from industry leaders and private providers will be available.

For example, Vinoth Nanda Kumaran—a 41-year-old in 2025—used SkillsFuture credits to shift careers from private hire driver to Business Intelligence Developer after earning a diploma in data engineering and continuing studies at SUSS.

And don’t forget: SkillsFuture Level-Up offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above $4,000 in credits plus training allowances up to $3,000 per month for full-time study over two years.

These aren’t just numbers or programs—they’re lifelines for real people navigating change.

Ultimately, these efforts are about something bigger: building an economy that remains dynamic, innovative, and full of opportunity for every Singaporean.

A dynamic economy means good jobs and better lives for all of us—not just a few.

Embracing AI supports Singapore’s goal of staying globally competitive. But more than that—we want to stay ahead through ideas, innovation, and unity.

AI is not just technology; it’s part of our future together.

Thank you.
