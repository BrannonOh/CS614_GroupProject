Good evening, everyone.

If you’ve planned a trip recently or interacted with any government service here in Singapore, you’ve likely already encountered artificial intelligence at work. AI is no longer a distant concept or a futuristic idea—it’s become an integral part of our daily lives. From helping us organize travel plans to streamlining public services, AI is quietly transforming how we live and work. This rapid advancement is nothing short of extraordinary. Just a few years ago, AI models struggled with basic reasoning tasks; today, they can pass rigorous professional exams like the bar exam—a feat once unimaginable. Our government is embracing this technology wholeheartedly. Take the ‘PAIR’ system, for example, deployed in government call centres—it transcribes conversations accurately in English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil and generates summaries that help civil servants serve you better. This allows our public officers to focus on more meaningful, citizen-facing work rather than routine tasks.

Having seen how AI is already woven into our daily lives and government, let's explore how it can boost productivity and create new value across sectors.

By thoughtfully integrating AI with deep human expertise, Singapore is carving out a unique competitive edge that attracts high-value investments and generates specialized, high-skilled jobs. The transformative power of AI isn’t reserved for multinational corporations alone; it’s accessible to all businesses—including our local SMEs—enabling them to enhance their capabilities and compete on a broader scale. In critical sectors such as logistics and manufacturing, AI acts as the brain behind the next generation of robotics and autonomous systems, revolutionizing key infrastructure that powers our economy. For instance, at Tuas Port, AI orchestrates a fleet of autonomous guided vehicles and yard cranes, dramatically boosting efficiency in one of the world’s busiest ports. Meanwhile, GE Vernova’s Singapore facility uses an AI-powered system to inspect turbine blades for microscopic defects invisible to the human eye—improving precision and safety. Even local SMEs like Q&M Dental Group are harnessing AI tools that analyze dental X-rays to assist dentists in detecting early signs of decay with greater accuracy—leading to better patient outcomes.

While AI drives innovation and growth, it also raises concerns about jobs, which we must address by looking at past experiences and future opportunities.

Singapore’s economic history shows us that technological shifts inevitably cause disruption but ultimately open pathways for workers to transition into higher-value, better-paying roles. Our national strategy has always been clear: proactively embrace technological change through systematic efforts to upskill our workforce for new economic realities. The key has never been just about training—it has involved fundamentally redesigning jobs in collaboration with workers so they can effectively leverage new technologies. Consider the National Computerisation Drive in the 1980s—a deliberate nationwide effort that trained an entire generation in IT skills and transformed Singapore into a knowledge-based economy. At PSA Ports today, crane operators have been upskilled into remote operations specialists who manage multiple automated cranes from control rooms—safer roles that require higher skills and offer better pay than before. Similarly, technicians who once performed manual assembly in advanced manufacturing have been retrained to operate complex robotic systems—significantly boosting their productivity and wages.

Building on this history, the government is prioritizing support for Singaporeans to thrive in the AI transition through targeted initiatives.

Our tripartite partnership between government, unions like NTUC, and employers forms the cornerstone of this strategy—proactively redesigning jobs for the AI era while ensuring training leads to concrete opportunities. We’re building a comprehensive support system for every worker: personalized career coaching and targeted job matching help individuals navigate career transitions with confidence. Importantly, we are making substantial investments in deep training for mid-career workers by providing significant financial support that enables major skill pivots. Take the SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme: it offers Singaporeans aged 40 and above a $4,000 credit top-up plus a monthly training allowance for selected full-time courses—making deep reskilling genuinely viable. One inspiring example is Vinoth Nanda Kumaran—a former private hire driver who used SkillsFuture credits to pursue a diploma in data engineering and successfully transitioned into a new career as a Business Intelligence Developer.

These efforts align with Singapore’s broader vision of a dynamic, innovative economy that benefits all citizens.

Our goal is not just to adopt AI but to shape its deployment so it creates a more prosperous and inclusive society for every Singaporean. Embracing AI marks the next chapter in our story—turning constraints into strengths and harnessing technology to build a better future for generations yet to come. Central to this vision is our National AI Strategy 2.0 which aims to triple our pool of core AI practitioners to 15,000 over the next five years—a concrete commitment to developing world-class local talent ready for this new era. Our success depends on collective courage—the courage to transform ourselves—and on our unwavering commitment to support one another as we journey forward together toward an innovative future grounded in unity.

Thank you.
