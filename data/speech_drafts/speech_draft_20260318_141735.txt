Fellow Singaporeans,

Not long ago, artificial intelligence—AI—was something distant, almost like science fiction. Today, it’s part of our everyday lives here in Singapore. This National Day Rally is a moment for us to reflect on how far we’ve come and where we’re headed. AI is no longer just an idea for the future; it’s already transforming how we live, work, and serve each other.

Look around. Many of you use AI when planning your travels—it recommends destinations and generates personalised itineraries tailored just for you. In our public services, AI is hard at work too. Our civil service call centres use AI to transcribe conversations in all four official languages and produce clear summaries. This lets officers spend more time focusing on what really matters: serving you better.

The pace of AI’s progress is striking. Back in 2020, AI struggled with basic math problems. Fast forward to 2025, and it can compete in the International Math Olympiad—performing at a gold medal level. That’s not some distant future; it’s happening right now.

Artificial intelligence will be one of the defining technologies of our era. Singapore must harness it—not just to keep up but to raise productivity, strengthen our competitiveness, and create better opportunities for every Singaporean.

So AI isn’t theoretical or far off anymore. The real question is: how do we use it to boost our economy and stay ahead in a world that’s changing faster than ever?

Singapore has always been good at combining technology with human expertise. This unique strength attracts high-quality investments and creates good jobs for our people—not just in big companies but also among small and medium enterprises.

Take Tuas Port: AI works hand-in-hand with robotics and autonomous machines to drive efficiency. At Changi Airport, they’re actively studying how AI-powered automation can improve operations even further. GE Vernova’s turbine repair facility uses AI to spot anomalies in turbine parts and alert human operators for precise diagnosis—helping them fix problems faster and better. Even SMEs like Q&M Dental benefit from AI by analysing dental X-rays more accurately, helping dentists detect fillings, impacted teeth, and tooth decay with greater precision.

These examples show how deploying AI across industries raises productivity and creates real value throughout our economy. But whenever new technology shakes things up, there’s a natural question: what will this mean for jobs?

It’s a fair question—and one we’ve faced before.

Technological change has always disrupted jobs at first but also opened doors to better ones.

In the 1980s, Singapore launched a nationwide computerisation drive that gave students and workers computer skills—and transformed our economy in the process. When the internet arrived in the 1990s, we adopted it quickly—and used it as a foundation for growth that propelled us forward.

Look at PSA today: crane operators were retrained to remotely supervise multiple cranes and vehicles at once. This not only boosted productivity but improved job quality—workers moved into higher-skilled roles with better pay.

We’ve been through this cycle before—and emerged stronger every time.

Just as we supported workers through past transformations, we must do so again as artificial intelligence reshapes industries now.

Our approach will remain people-centred. The government will work closely with NTUC and unions to redesign jobs and equip workers with the new skills they need to thrive.

We’re stepping up efforts to help Singaporeans access good jobs—expanding job matching services, holding more career fairs at tertiary institutions, and launching a new town-level job matching initiative led by Community Development Councils (CDCs).

The SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme will also grow—covering more part-time training allowance claims and adding courses from industry leaders and private training providers.

For Singaporeans aged 40 and above, SkillsFuture offers $4,000 in credits plus training allowances up to $3,000 per month for full-time study lasting up to 24 months—making it easier to reskill or upskill when needed.

Take Vinoth Nanda Kumaran as an example: at 41 years old in 2025, he transitioned from being a private hire driver to a Business Intelligence Developer after using SkillsFuture credits for a diploma in data engineering—and continued his studies in Business Analytics at SUSS. That kind of career shift shows what’s possible with the right support.

Ultimately, these efforts are about something bigger: building an economy that stays dynamic, innovative, and full of opportunity for every Singaporean.

A dynamic economy is one that creates good jobs—and better lives—for all of us here today and those who come after us. Embracing AI isn’t about chasing trends blindly; it’s about staying globally competitive by leading through ideas, innovation—and unity among all Singaporeans.

We face tough challenges ahead. But if we confront them head-on—with clear focus on people first—we can harness this defining technology of our era for every Singaporean today...and tomorrow.

Thank you.
